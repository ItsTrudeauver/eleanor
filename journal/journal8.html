A NIGHT OF CHAOS: 400 MEMBERS OF "WHITE KNIGHTS ORGANIZATION" CAUGHT AT THE EYE HEADQUARTERS, BEACON TOWER

WKO saw swift defeat after attempting to topple The Beacon Tower, according to on-scene reports

In Other News

Local Government News:The Eye continues expanding influence, now involved in discussions of infrastructure and finances.

Market Report:TYE stocks raised by another 7%, marking an unprecedented streak of quarterly growth

Weather Forecast: Humid and clear, fluctuating around 59°...

WKO leader Mara Urbenshelck and nearly 400 other members invaded The Beacon Tower at approximately 1:37 AM, per CCTVs on scene

"They had number, weapons, anger and drive, and they caused tangible damage. However, the security division have trained and simulated various scenarios, and we managed to minimize damage."
Patrick Anthorne, Head of Security

The WKO have been at war with The Eye ever since their first batch of military contracts half a decade ago, with constant vandalism, online harrassment and protests, some violent. WKO, known for its steadfast stance against technological acceleration, firmly stood up against The Eye's acquisitions of allying company, citing anti-trust and fair competition laws, as well as environmental procedures as sources for their frustrations. Their movement gained sustained traction and support from various groups and individuals, reports by Civic Polls.

Chaos broke, as WKO took mass physical action with 400 personnel and leader Mara Urbenshelck invading The Beacon Tower, an important headquarter for The Eye. However, the invasion was subdued within the hour. By 2:42 AM, all WKO members were detained or incapacitated, sustaining moderate to severe injuries. Eye security personnel also suffered "tangible losses," according to internal reports, but no specifics were disclosed. Medical teams, both independent and Eye-affiliated were deployed on-site to prevent fatalities. Despite the group's aggression, The Eye has chosen not to press charges. Mara Urbenshelck remains in temporary detainment for questioning, and a company spokesperson assured that all individuals will receive "fair and lawful" treatment. Explaining The Eye's course of action, Mr. Joseph P. Law, Board member, stated:

"We do not agree with their methods, but their anxieties and cause resonate with many, thus prompting us to approach discussions from unexplored angles. We need to assure others that The Eye is not above criticism. We welcome dialogue.

Officials continue investigating the extent of the damage, but early reports suggest no core research sectors were compromised. Maintenance teams are assessing structural damage in the breached sectors, with affected areas expected to resume function by the end of the week.
